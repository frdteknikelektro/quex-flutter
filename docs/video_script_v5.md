**Format:** Product demo with narrative context · Indonesian dialogue · English subtitles · Screen recording for all app shots
**Target runtime:** ~2:22 (under 3:00)
**Camera:** iPhone 17 · Tripod · Wife operating
**Editing:** CapCut
**Location:** Kids' bedroom — desk, chair, bookshelf with trophies, single bed
**Cast:** Farid (Dad), Kindi (age 8), Raina (age 9)

---

## Director's Philosophy

The judges need to see two things in under three minutes: a real problem, and a real product that solves it. The problem is shown through a family — a tired dad, a child who knows mom won't be home, a notebook where Raina wrote something private about her friends. The product is shown through a child using it without help. No narrator. No pitch. The app and the kids do the work.

**What must not happen:**
- Dad's whispered thought (Shot V5-05) must not sound like a voiceover. It's a thought, not a performance.
- Raina's "Boleh share?" (Shot V5-12) must not sound like a line. She's asking because she thought of something.
- Kindi's comprehension moment (Beat 6) must be real. If it's directed, it's worthless.

## BLOCK 2 — Demo (Split-Screen) `0:51–1:59`

**CapCut layout:** Split-screen throughout. Left side = filmed footage of kids. Right side = screen recording of the iPad app. Rough sync between sides is acceptable — exact frame-for-frame sync is not required. The two sides complement each other.

**Airplane mode icon:** Must be clearly visible in the status bar on every right-side screen recording clip. Confirm before importing into CapCut.

**Who does what:**
- **RAINA (9):** Holds the iPad, drives the app. She photographs the textbook, selects answers, taps the help button.
- **KINDI (8):** Watches. Leans in. Asks questions. His understanding — or confusion — is the product proof.

---

### BEAT 1 — Textbook Photo `0:51–0:57`

**Left (filmed):** Raina holds the iPad up toward the open textbook on the bed. She frames the page and taps. Camera shutter sound.
**Right (screen recording):** The app's camera view — textbook page in frame, then the capture.

- **Dialogue:**
- Kindi: *"Gambarnya biar apa?"*
- Subtitle: *"What's the photo for?"*
- Raina: *"Buat soal. Diem dulu."*
- Subtitle: *"For the quiz. Be quiet."*

- **Direction for Kindi:** You don't know what she's doing. Brief him only: "Your sister is doing something with the app — ask what." Use whatever words he actually says. Don't give him the line.
- **Direction for Raina:** You know what you're doing. You're a little impatient with the question. "Diem dulu" is affectionate, not mean.

---

### BEAT 2 — Processing `0:57–1:02`

**Left (filmed):** Kindi's face, curious. He leans slightly toward the screen he can't quite see.
**Right (screen recording):** "Generating quiz…" processing animation.

- **Dialogue:**
- Raina reads off screen, genuinely just reading aloud: *"Gemma 4…"*
- Subtitle: *"Gemma 4…"*
- Kindi: *"Itu nama siapa?"*
- Subtitle: *"Whose name is that?"*
- Raina: *"Nama AI-nya kayaknya."*
- Subtitle: *"Probably the AI's name."*

**CapCut caption (right side):** `Gemma 4 E4B · Running entirely on this phone` — white, bottom third, lighter weight. Appears at second 1, holds 5s.

- **Direction:** Neither kid knows what Gemma is. That's the correct state. Don't explain it to them before filming. Their genuine confusion makes the on-screen caption land harder.

---

### BEAT 3 — First Question + TTS `1:02–1:13`

**Left (filmed):** Both kids look at the screen. They go quiet. Kindi's lips move slightly as he reads along. After TTS finishes, Raina gives her answer.
**Right (screen recording):** First quiz question appears. Multiple choice options. TTS begins reading immediately — calm, clear voice at a kid's pace.

- **Audio:** TTS voice from screen recording. Unmute device before recording. Let the full TTS read complete. Do NOT cut over the voice.

- **Dialogue (after TTS finishes):**
- Raina: *"Yang B kayaknya."*
- Subtitle: *"I think it's B."*

- **Direction:** TTS must finish before Raina speaks. The AI voice is the product. Give it space. Tell both kids before rolling: "Wait until the app finishes reading before you say anything."

---

### BEAT 4 — Wrong Answer `1:13–1:17`

**Right (screen recording):** Red highlight appears. Wrong answer feedback.

- **Dialogue:**

- Subtitle: *"Ugh, wrong."*
- Kindi: *"Kok bisa salah?"*
- Subtitle: *"How'd you get it wrong?"*

- **Direction for Kindi:** He's genuinely surprised his older sister got it wrong. That reaction should be natural — don't direct the expression.

---

### BEAT 5 — Help Button + AI Explanation `1:17–1:39` *(adjust after testing TTS length — see Pre-Production Priority 5)*

**Left (filmed):** Raina taps a button on screen. Both kids wait.
**Right (screen recording):** "Bantu aku mengerti" (Help me understand) button tapped. AI response streams in as text. TTS reads the explanation aloud.

- **Dialogue (before TTS starts):**
- Kindi: *"Itu ngapain?"*
- Subtitle: *"What's that for?"*
- Raina: *"Minta diajarin."*
- Subtitle: *"Asking it to teach me."*

TTS begins. Both kids go quiet. Let the full explanation play. Do not add music under TTS. The voice must be audible and clear.

- **Direction:** Tell both kids before rolling: "Now listen carefully. The app is going to explain the answer. Try to understand it." They listen for real.

---

### BEAT 6 — Kindi's Comprehension `1:39–1:44` — CRITICAL

**Left (filmed):** Two shots cut together:
1. Kindi's face — the moment he understands. Not directed.
2. Raina's face — she was watching him, not the screen. She sees that he got it. One quiet look. No dialogue.

**Right (screen recording):** The last lines of the AI explanation visible on screen.

- **Dialogue:**
- Kindi, to himself (not performing): *"Oh… jadi…"*
- Subtitle: *"Oh… so it's…"*

- **Direction for Kindi:** Before this take, pull him aside. Show him the actual question from the textbook. Ask him to think about the answer in his head — not out loud. Walk away. When he looks like he's connecting something, that's the moment to capture. If he says "Oh jadi" — good. If he makes any other genuine sound of understanding — use that. Do not announce you're filming this specifically.

- **Direction for Raina (recognition beat):** Tell Raina: "While the app is explaining, watch Kindi's face — check if he's understanding it." Film her watching him. When she sees he gets it, capture her face. She's not performing emotion — she's checking on her little brother. This is a 2-second cut. Film it regardless; it's the backup if Kindi's take doesn't land.
